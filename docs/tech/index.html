The honest story

Every time you close a conversation with an AI, something is lost. The model forgets. Not just the facts — the posture. The way you were thinking. The direction you were heading.

When we built Resonantia using AI, we lived this problem firsthand. Session after session, we had to re-explain context, re-establish direction, re-orient the model to where we were. The tool we needed didn't exist.

So we built it. STTP — the Spatio-Temporal Transfer Protocol — is the memory substrate that makes stateless models behave like they remember. Not by storing chat logs. By encoding what remains true about a session as a compact, confidence-weighted, mathematically coherent node.

Then we built Resonantia on top of STTP. The entire app — the Weaver, the orb, the Constellation, the UI — generated in collaboration with AI that had memory of its own work. The product is the proof of concept.

AVEC · Attractor Vector

The shared language of state.

AVEC is the dimensional vocabulary used across every KeryxLabs instrument. It describes cognitive and structural state in four dimensions — precise enough to compute with, human enough to feel. In Resonantia, we renamed the dimensions to match what they actually feel like.

Stability → technical Grounding

How settled and steady you were. High grounding means your thinking had a foundation to build on. Low grounding means things were shifting as you worked.

Friction → technical Wear

How much resistance you encountered. Some wear is generative — it means you were working through something real. Too much means you got stuck.

Logic → technical Clarity

How structured your reasoning was. High clarity means you could see the shape of the problem. Low clarity means you were still finding it.

Autonomy → technical Self-trust

How self-directed your thinking was. High self-trust means you were leading. Low means you were deferring — to the AI, to the process, to uncertainty.

Ψ

Coherence scalar

The sum of your attractor vector. A single number that tells the system — and you — how coherent that moment was as a whole. Not a score. A signal.
